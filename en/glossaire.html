The vocabulary

المُعْجَم

The words of Mullā Ṣadrā

Each concept always appears in the same form: vocalized Arabic, transliteration, French and English. Unfold it for the root, the literal sense and the precise sense in Ṣadrā. Little by little, one learns the vocabulary.

9 terms

الْوُجُود al-wujūd al-woujoud
existence, being l'existence, l'être

The act of existing, that by which there is something rather than nothing.

In-depth
Root w-j-d, “to find, be found”. Literal sense: effective presence. In Ṣadrā: the only fundamental reality (aṣīl); essence is but its limit. Basis of aṣālat al-wujūd and of gradation.
الْمَاهِيَّة al-māhiyya al-mâhiyya
essence, quiddity l'essence, la quiddité

What a thing is: its definable nature, the answer to “what is it?”.

In-depth
Root from mā huwa, “what is it”. In Ṣadrā: it is iʿtibāriyya, posited by the mind; real as a limit, not as the ground of the real. To be distinguished from wujūd.
أَصَالَةُ الْوُجُودِ aṣālat al-wujūd assâlat al-woujoud
primacy of existence primauté de l'existence

What constitutes the reality of anything is its act of existing, not its essence.

In-depth
Root ʾ-ṣ-l, “root, foundation”. Sense: existence is aṣīl, essence iʿtibārī. Pivot of the whole system. → see the full notion
التَّشْكِيك tashkīk al-wujūd tachkîk
gradation of existence gradation analogique de l'existence

Existence is a single reality said in degrees of intensity, not a genus shared equally.

In-depth
Root sh-k-k, “to grade, scale”. In Ṣadrā: perfection/deficiency, strength/weakness are interior to existence. Direct daughter of aṣālat al-wujūd; opens substantial motion.
أَصِيل aṣīl açîl
fundamental, principial fondamental, principiel

What has reality by itself, without borrowing it from anything else.

In-depth
Root ʾ-ṣ-l. Sense: what stems from the root, originary. Opposed to iʿtibārī. In Ṣadrā, only existence is aṣīl.
اِعْتِبَارِيّ iʿtibārī iʿtibârî
of the mind, mentally posited de raison, considéré par l'esprit

What has reality only in and through the mind's consideration.

In-depth
Root ʿ-b-r, “to consider”. Opposed to aṣīl. In Ṣadrā, essence is iʿtibāriyya: a mental profile, not a ground.
الْحَرَكَةُ الْجَوْهَرِيَّة al-ḥaraka al-jawhariyya
substantial motion le mouvement substantiel

Substance itself is not fixed beneath changing accidents: it renews itself inwardly.

In-depth
Sense: the motion of substance (jawhar), not only of its accidents. Made possible by the gradation of existence; grounds the soul's becoming and the return.
النَّفْس al-nafs an-nafs
the soul l'âme

In Ṣadrā: bodily in its origination, spiritual in its subsistence.

In-depth
Root n-f-s, “breath, self”. Formula: jismāniyyat al-ḥudūth, rūḥāniyyat al-baqāʾ. The soul is born with the body and rises, degree by degree, to the return.
الْمَعَاد al-maʿād al-maʿâd
the return, resurrection le retour, la résurrection

The end of the four journeys: the return of being to its source.

In-depth
Root ʿ-w-d, “to return”. In Ṣadrā: resurrection follows from the system (primacy, gradation, substantial motion, the soul's becoming), not added from outside as a belief.
