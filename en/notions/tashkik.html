Journey I · Existence — al-wujūd

التَّشْكِيكُ فِي الْوُجُودِ

tashkīk al-wujūd  — tachkîk al-woujoud

Gradation of Existence  ·  La gradation de l'existence

Level I — Grasp the notion

In one minute

In one sentence: existence is a single reality — but it is said in degrees, more or less intense, and this gradation is what makes the whole hierarchy of the real.
Vocalized
التَّشْكِيكُ فِي الْوُجُودِ
Transliteration
tashkīk al-wujūd
Pronunciation
tachkîk al-woujoud
French
gradation analogique de l'existence
English
gradation (systematic ambiguity) of existence

Fundamental intuition. If the real is existence (وُجُود) and not essence, by what do beings differ? By nothing other than existence: by more and less of it. The real is one light, at different intensities.

Level II — Understand by experience

One light, a thousand intensities

Think of light: a candle's and the sun's are not two different natures. It is the same light — one faint, one intense. They differ not by what they are, but by degree. Existence, for Ṣadrā, is like this: from the poorest of beings up to the Necessary Being, a single reality, graded.

It is not size that grows, but intensity of being. No superadded essence: the degree lies in existence itself.
Between the ember and the blaze there are not two fires: there is the same fire, at two degrees of ardour. So it is with all that is.

What it changes: the world is neither a dust of equal beings nor a collection of separate natures. It is a continuous ladder — and the mind can climb it, from the sensible toward the divine.

Level III — Follow the reasoning

What unites is what distinguishes

Gradation hides a formidable difficulty, which Ṣadrā meets head-on.

The question examined

Since existence alone is real (أَصِيل) and essence grounds nothing, by what do the countless beings differ?

The terms used

“Tashkīk” does not mean doubt: it is a term's applying by degrees — neither equally to all (univocal), nor by mere accident of name (equivocal), but modulated.

The decisive distinction

In an ordinary genus, what unites (the genus “animal”) differs from what distinguishes (the differentia “rational”). Here, no: that by which beings resemble one another — existence — is the very thing by which they differ.

The march of the argument

Beings share existence; yet they differ; and no essence can add reality (previous level); therefore their difference comes from nothing external to existence — it is interior to it: it is degree.

The difficulty he resolves

Objection: “if a single reality, then all is identical, multiplicity vanishes.” Answer: gradation preserves real multiplicity — not as separate things, but as degrees of intensity of one same being.

The conclusion

Existence is a single graded reality — “al-tashkīk al-khāṣṣī”: a gradation where more and less fall within the very nature of what is graded.

The immediate consequence

A continuous hierarchy opens, from the least existent to the Necessary Being. And if a being can hold more existence, then it can intensify: the door of substantial motion opens.

⟡ Indispensable distinctions

  • the unity of the concept of existence ≠ the gradation of the reality (one notion, a thousand degrees)
  • degree (intensity of being) ≠ quantity (size, number)
  • “that by which we share” = “that by which we differ” — proper to existence alone
  • gradation in reality ≠ mere ambiguity of the word

✕ Common difficulties · risks of misreading

  • Taking gradation for a quantitative difference. It is an intensity of being, not a size.
  • Thinking the degrees are separate species. It is a single continuous reality.
  • Reducing tashkīk to the ambiguity of a word. The gradation is in the real, not only in language.
  • Fearing that unity erases multiplicity. It grounds it, as degrees of one same light.

◈ Internal questions — and their resolution

How can what unites be what divides?

Because existence is not a genus. A genus stays indifferent to its species; existence, by contrast, is each degree. Its commonality and its difference are one and the same reality, taken at various intensities.

Does gradation not lead to monism (all is One)?

No: the degrees are really distinct. The unity is that of a living hierarchy, not of an undifferentiated mass. The many is saved as degrees.

How can a simple reality carry degrees?

More-and-less is not added from outside: it is interior to existence, as intensity is interior to light. Simplicity and degrees do not exclude one another.

Consequence in his system: gradation is the link that turns the primacy of existence into an ordered world — hierarchy of causes, intensification of substances, ascent of the soul, return to the source. See Level V.

Level IV — Enter the text

The founding passage

Reference: al-Ḥikma al-mutaʿāliya fī l-asfār al-ʿaqliyya al-arbaʿa Journey I · Part 1 · al-marḥala al-ūlā (on existence) ⚠ page & edition to verify against the PDF (OCR to be installed)

The same statement grounds both primacy and gradation: it is the bridge-text between the two doctrines.

الوجود حقيقة عينية واحدة بسيطة لا اختلاف بين أفرادها لذاتها إلا بالكمال والنقص والشدة والضعف

al-wujūdu ḥaqīqatun ʿayniyyatun wāḥidatun basīṭatun lā ikhtilāfa bayna afrādihā li-dhātihā illā bi-l-kamāli wa-l-naqṣi wa-l-shiddati wa-l-ḍaʿf
French translation (original)

« L'existence est une réalité concrète, une et simple ; ses individus ne diffèrent pas entre eux par leur essence même, sinon par la perfection et le manque, l'intensité et la faiblesse. »

English translation (original)

“Existence is a single, simple, concrete reality; its instances do not differ from one another in their very essence, save by perfection and deficiency, intensity and weakness.”

Commentary — gradation, word by word

وَاحِدَةٌ بَسِيطَةٌ
one and simpleA single nature, without parts — the ground of unity that gradation must respect.
لَا اخْتِلَافَ بَيْنَ أَفْرَادِهَا لِذَاتِهَا
no difference of essenceNothing external separates beings: the difference can only be internal.
إِلَّا بِالْكَمَالِ وَالنَّقْصِ
perfection / deficiencyThe first axis of gradation: to have more or less being.
وَالشِّدَّةِ وَالضَّعْفِ
intensity / weaknessThe second axis: the same existence, more or less intense. This is the very definition of tashkīk.
Role of the passage: the last four words — perfection, deficiency, intensity, weakness — are the doctrine of gradation. Ṣadrā does not add tashkīk to primacy: he reads it in the same sentence.

Level V — The notion within the whole system

The ladder that makes the journey possible

Gradation is no isolated thesis: it is the architecture the primacy of existence called for. Without it, the real would stay flat.

Where it appears · how it is introduced

Right after primacy, in the first stage of the first journey. It is its immediate extension: one same sentence carries them both.

How its function shifts by subject

Metaphysics: it orders the real into a hierarchy. Physics: it makes it thinkable that a being gains existence (substantial motion). Theology: it culminates in the Necessary Being, supreme degree and source. Science of the soul: it explains how the soul can rise degree by degree.

What it depends on · what it enables

It depends entirely on the primacy of existence. It enables nearly all that follows: causality, nature, theology, soul, return.

Its role in the unity of the four journeys

It is the very ladder of the journey: without degrees, no ascent, no return.
