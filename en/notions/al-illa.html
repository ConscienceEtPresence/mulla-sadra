Journey I · Causality

العِلَّةُ وَالمَعْلُول

al-ʿilla wa-l-maʿlūl  — al-ʿilla wa-l-maʿloûl

Cause and Effect  ·  La cause et l'effet

Level I — Grasp the notion

In one minute

In one sentence: the effect is not a thing the cause “manufactures” beside itself; it is only a degree, a mode of the existence that flows from the cause — a ray of its light.
Vocalized
العِلَّةُ وَالمَعْلُول
Transliteration
al-ʿilla wa-l-maʿlūl
Pronunciation
al-ʿilla wa-l-maʿloûl
French
la cause et l'effet
English
cause and effect

Fundamental intuition. Since existence alone is real (وُجُود) and it is graded (تَشْكِيك), to cause cannot mean “to produce one more thing”: it means to manifest a lesser degree of the same being.

Level II — Understand by experience

The ray and the sun

Look at a sunbeam. It is not a thing the sun made and then released to live on its own: the ray is the sun-shining-here. Hide the sun, and the ray goes out at once — because it never had a being of its own. The effect, for Ṣadrā, stands thus to its cause.

“Existence is a light by which the dark-natured quiddities appear” (Ṣadrā). The effect is not made: it is manifested.
We usually think the world is a chain of independent things pushing one another. For Ṣadrā, it is one light manifesting itself in degrees.

What it changes: to cause is not to manufacture (to posit one more independent thing) but to manifest (to make a degree of being appear). Causation becomes another name for manifestation.

Level III — Follow the reasoning

The effect, a mode of being

Ṣadrā draws the rigorous consequence of his first two doctrines.

The question examined

What is the effect (مَعْلُول) relative to its cause? A separate existent merely “linked” to it? Or something else?

The terms used

Cause (ʿilla), effect (maʿlūl), agent (jāʿil), the “making” (jaʿl), manifestation (ẓuhūr).

The decisive distinction

The “making” (jaʿl) never bears on the essence: one does not manufacture a quiddity. It bears only on a degree of existence. The cause of the essence and the cause of the existence are not the same question.

The march of the argument

Existence alone is real; it is graded; therefore what the agent “posits” can only be a degree of existence, not an autonomous thing. The effect has no being beside its cause: its being is its dependence.

The difficulty he resolves

Objection: “then the effect does not really exist?” Answer: it really exists — as the ray is real — but with a dependent existence; real in being, poor in independence.

The conclusion

“the effect of the agent is nothing but a mode of existence, a degree of manifestation” (Chapter 25). To cause is to manifest a degree.

The immediate consequence

The whole cosmos becomes the graded manifestation of a single existence. The first cause is no longer the first link of a chain: it is the light of which all else is a ray — the door of theology (the Necessary Being).

⟡ Indispensable distinctions

  • the cause of the essence ≠ the cause of the existence (jaʿl bears only on existence)
  • the effect as an independent thing ≠ the effect as a mode / degree of existence
  • to manufacture (posit a thing beside) ≠ to manifest (make a degree appear)
  • succession in time ≠ dependence in existence

✕ Common difficulties · risks of misreading

  • Thinking the effect is an autonomous being the cause “pushes.” It is a degree of being flowing from it.
  • Thinking to cause is to precede in time. It is a dependence in existence, not a succession.
  • Thinking the cause “manufactures” an essence. Jaʿl bears only on a degree of existence.
  • Thinking “dependent” means “unreal.” The ray is real — but has no being of its own.

◈ Internal questions — and their resolution

If the effect is only a mode of the cause, does it really exist?

Yes, really: but with a dependent existence. As the ray's light is truly real without being autonomous, the effect is real without existing by itself.

What then distinguishes the effect from its cause?

The degree. The effect is a lesser degree of the same light: gradation (tashkīk) again, applied to dependence.

Where does dependence stop?

At the Necessary Being, the only one to exist by itself. All else — everything caused — is but a ray of that single light.

Consequence in his system: causation is not manufacture but a graded manifestation of being. It directly extends primacy and gradation, and opens theology and the return. See Level V.

Level V — The notion within the whole system

The descending movement of being

Cause is the descending slope of the journey: from the source toward the many. It prepares the ascending slope — the return.

Where it appears · how it is introduced

In Part 2 (causality), after the study of essence and unity. Chapter 25 “unveils its secrets”: cause is there reduced to manifestation.

How its function shifts by subject

Metaphysics: the ontological dependence of the effect. Theology: the First as cause of all being — the light of which all is a ray. Cosmology: the possibles as mirrors of the Real's manifestation (Chapter 33).

What it depends on · what it enables

It depends on primacy and gradation. It enables theology (the Necessary Being as source) and, through it, the whole arc of descent and return.

Its role in the unity of the four journeys

It is the descent of being from its source — the movement the later journeys will climb back.

Journey Ithe effect is a degree of existence
Journey IIthis degree moves and intensifies
Journey IIIthe first cause: the Necessary Being
Journey IVthe ray climbs back to its source: the return
